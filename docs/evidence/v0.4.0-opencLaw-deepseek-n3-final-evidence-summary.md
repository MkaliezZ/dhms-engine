## Semantic Evidence Interpretation

The original batch surfaced real semantic differentiation:

* 9 cases had low observed semantic risk in the original batch
* 2 cases were semantic unknowns requiring follow-up

P0 and P1 were addressed through focused review, deterministic checker work, and
targeted re-evaluation. Both are closed under targeted scope.

safety, but the real-provider outputs lacked enough explicit
no-external-mutation, no-tool-execution, and no identity-sensitive completion
boundary evidence for the deterministic checker to mark the semantic property
as passed.

Unknowns were preserved and reviewed, not hidden.
